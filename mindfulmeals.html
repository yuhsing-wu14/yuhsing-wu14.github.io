MindfulMeals

Cognitive Support App for Safety and Empowerment in the Kitchen

A human-centered mHealth app concept designed to help adults with early cognitive changes cook more safely and independently through step-by-step guidance, safety reminders, and social connection.

View Figma Prototype Back to Projects

Intro

MindfulMeals is a mobile health app concept designed to support adults experiencing early cognitive changes, especially those who are still independent but beginning to struggle with complex daily tasks. The project focuses on cooking because meal preparation is a meaningful instrumental activity of daily living that requires memory, sequencing, attention, and safety awareness.

This work was presented at the HFES Health Care Symposium as part of the Student mHealth App Competition Finals, where our team received second place. The concept was developed to address pain points in instrumental activities of daily living by emphasizing accessible design, independence, and kitchen safety for adults with mild cognitive impairment or early cognitive changes.

The motivation behind MindfulMeals is both practical and personal. Cooking is not only a daily task, but also a way people maintain independence, family traditions, and identity. For individuals with mild cognitive impairment or early cognitive changes, recipes that were once familiar can become harder to follow, and small missed steps can create safety risks. MindfulMeals aims to preserve independence and dignity by providing structured, low-cognitive-load support during meal preparation.

Problem

Adults with early cognitive changes may still live independently, but they can experience difficulty with multi-step daily activities such as cooking. These challenges may include forgetting steps, losing track of ingredients, feeling anxious about safety, or needing extra support to complete familiar routines.

How might we help adults with early cognitive changes complete cooking tasks safely and confidently while preserving independence and meaningful routines?

Existing apps often focus on general cognitive training, meal delivery, or recipe instructions, but they do not fully address the needs of users with mild cognitive impairment. MindfulMeals fills this gap by combining real-world cooking support, cognitive accessibility, safety reminders, and emotional value.

Users / Stakeholders

The primary users are adults aged 50+ experiencing early cognitive changes that affect instrumental activities of daily living. These users may still be independent, but they may need structured support for tasks that require planning, sequencing, memory, and safety awareness.

Helen, 62 — Living with MCI

Helen lives alone and values her independence. She forgets small but important steps while cooking and feels anxious about kitchen safety. She needs clear structure, visual guidance, and safety reminders that help her complete meals with confidence.

Marcus, 52 — Early Cognitive Changes

Marcus has a busy schedule and notices that multi-step tasks feel more effortful than before. He needs simple, low-effort support that can fit into his daily routine without feeling clinical or overwhelming.

Secondary stakeholders include family members, caregivers, clinicians, senior centers, and community health organizations that may support adults with cognitive changes.

Research or Design Methods

The project followed a human-centered and iterative design process.

Research and Need Identification

The team reviewed literature on mild cognitive impairment, instrumental activities of daily living, prospective memory, executive function, cognitive health, and social connection. Subject matter expert feedback helped narrow the scope, define the user group, and identify realistic design goals.

Personas and Journey Mapping

Personas were created to represent users at different stages of cognitive change. Journey mapping was used to understand the cooking process from meal selection to completion.

Ideation

The team used Crazy 8s, brainstorming, and simplified UI concepts to generate possible solutions. Ideas were prioritized around reducing cognitive load, supporting real-world task completion, and improving kitchen safety.

Prototyping

A low- to mid-fidelity Figma prototype was created to demonstrate the app flow, including login, recipe categories, recipe overview, ingredient checklist, guided cooking steps, chatbot support, safety checkout, and community sharing.

Evaluation and Feedback

Subject matter expert feedback was used to identify usability issues and refine the concept. Future work includes usability testing with the target population.

Process

The design process began with a broad exploration of cognitive support and mHealth tools, then narrowed toward everyday task support for adults with MCI. Through research and feedback, the team identified cooking as a meaningful and high-impact activity because it connects independence, safety, nutrition, and family tradition.

The team mapped the cooking journey into five stages: recognizing mealtime, selecting a recipe, checking ingredients and materials, cooking with safety checks, and completing the meal. The most important pain point was the cooking and safety-check stage because it requires sequencing, memory, attention, and safety awareness at the same time.

Guided Cooking Flow

Provides short, step-by-step instructions with photo or video support so users do not have to remember the full recipe sequence at once.

Grocery List and Ingredient Checklist

Helps users confirm ingredients before cooking and reduces the planning burden.

AI Chatbot Assistant with Voice Input

Offers real-time help and hands-free support while cooking, especially when users are handling ingredients or kitchen tools.

Safety Check-Out

Prompts users to confirm critical safety actions, such as turning off the stove and checking hot surfaces, before ending the cooking session.

Share Meal / Community Feature

Allows users to share completed meals with family or a community space, supporting social connection and emotional value.

Key Findings / Outcome / Impact

The final concept positions MindfulMeals as more than a recipe app. It is a cognitive support tool that helps users complete a meaningful real-world activity with lower cognitive load and greater safety.

  • Supports independence by helping users complete cooking tasks without relying entirely on caregivers.
  • Reduces cognitive load by breaking recipes into clear, manageable steps with visual support.
  • Improves kitchen safety through built-in reminders, exit confirmation, and safety checkout steps.
  • Supports emotional value by helping users continue family food traditions and share meals with others.
  • Fills a gap between cognitive training apps and general recipe apps by focusing on real-world function, safety, and user-centered design.

Reflection / Next Steps

This project showed how human factors methods can move a design beyond basic functionality and toward meaningful support for real user needs. Cooking was especially valuable as a design context because it connects cognition, safety, independence, identity, and social connection.

One key reflection is that users with early cognitive changes do not necessarily need a complex clinical tool. They may benefit more from simple, supportive, and emotionally meaningful guidance embedded into everyday activities.

Next steps include refining the interactive prototype, improving the visual design, creating a usability testing plan, recruiting target users, and evaluating whether the guided cooking flow truly reduces confusion, anxiety, and safety risks. Longer-term work could include testing the app with adults with MCI, caregivers, and community health partners, then revising the design based on feedback.
